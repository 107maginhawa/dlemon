# Periodontal Charting — Industry + Clinical Standards Research

> Research compiled 2026-06-02 for dentalemon perio-charting module. Evidence-backed; inline source URLs per claim.

## Industry-standard benchmark

### Clinical perio charting data points

- **6-point probing depths per tooth.** Standard of care is a "continuous walking stroke around the entire circumference" recording **six sites per tooth** (mesiobuccal, mid-buccal, distobuccal, mesiolingual, mid-lingual, distolingual), using a probe with 1mm markings and ~0.20–0.25 N (approx. 20–25 g) of light force; readings between markings are rounded up. — https://www.todaysrdh.com/a-dental-hygienists-guide-to-periodontal-probing-and-charting/
- **Bleeding on Probing (BOP).** Recorded per-site (the specific site where it occurs), assessed ~30 seconds after probing to distinguish true inflammation from probing trauma. Periodontal **health = <10% BOP**; **localized gingivitis = 10–30%** bleeding sites; **generalized gingivitis = >30%**. BOP should be recorded even when probing depth is ≤3mm. — https://www.todaysrdh.com/a-dental-hygienists-guide-to-periodontal-probing-and-charting/ , https://dimensionsofdentalhygiene.com/questions/periodontal-charting/
- **Gingival margin / recession.** Recorded as the position of the gingival margin relative to the CEJ — recession is a negative offset (margin apical to CEJ), and a positive value when tissue is coronal to the CEJ (e.g. overgrowth/edema). — https://www.opendental.com/manual/perio.html , https://pocketdentistry.com/periodontal-measurements-indicators-of-disease-and-conditions/
- **Clinical Attachment Level (CAL).** The position of attached tissue at the base of the pocket, measured from the fixed **CEJ** (not the gingival margin). Calculated per-site: if gingival margin (GM) is **at CEJ**, CAL = probing depth (PD); if **recession** present, CAL = PD + (CEJ→GM distance); if **GM covers/coronal to CEJ**, CAL = PD − (CEJ→GM distance). CAL is the truest measure of attachment loss because it is independent of margin position. — https://www.todaysrdh.com/a-dental-hygienists-guide-to-periodontal-probing-and-charting/ , https://pocketdentistry.com/periodontal-measurements-indicators-of-disease-and-conditions/
- **Tooth mobility (Miller classification).** N = normal physiologic; **Grade 1** = slight pathologic mobility beyond normal; **Grade 2** = moderate, >1mm horizontal displacement; **Grade 3** = severe, both horizontal and vertical (depressible) movement. — https://www.todaysrdh.com/a-dental-hygienists-guide-to-periodontal-probing-and-charting/
- **Furcation involvement (Glickman grades).** **Grade I** = probe enters furcation concavity, interradicular bone intact; **Grade II** = probe enters but does not pass through (cul-de-sac); **Grade III** = through-and-through, still soft-tissue covered; **Grade IV** = through-and-through with gingival recession exposing the furcation. Hamp's horizontal classification is widely used alongside Glickman: **Class 1** = <3mm horizontal attachment loss; **Class 2** = >3mm but not through-and-through; **Class 3** = through-and-through. — https://www.todaysrdh.com/a-dental-hygienists-guide-to-periodontal-probing-and-charting/ , https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6162379/
- **Plaque index, suppuration, calculus.** Captured as discrete per-site markers (presence/absence) typically rendered as colored dots above the probing numbers; plaque score is reported as a whole-mouth percentage. Suppuration (purulent exudate) is an inflammatory marker recorded per-site. — https://www.opendental.com/manual/perio.html
- **MGJ / keratinized & attached tissue.** Mucogingival junction (MGJ) position is recorded on maxillary and mandibular surfaces; width of keratinized/attached gingiva is derived from GM and MGJ positions. — https://www.opendental.com/manual/perio.html

### 2017 AAP/EFP classification — Staging & Grading (numeric thresholds)

The 2017 World Workshop (co-presented by AAP + EFP) replaced "chronic/aggressive" with a **multidimensional staging (severity/complexity) and grading (progression rate) system**, plus an extent descriptor. — https://www.perio.org/research-science/2017-classification-of-periodontal-and-peri-implant-diseases-and-conditions/ , https://aap.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/JPER.18-0157

**Staging** — primary determinant is interdental CAL at the site of greatest loss (use RBL if CAL unavailable); tooth loss and complexity factors can shift the stage upward:

| Factor | Stage I | Stage II | Stage III | Stage IV |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Interdental CAL (worst site) | 1–2 mm | 3–4 mm | ≥5 mm | ≥5 mm |
| Radiographic bone loss (RBL) | Coronal third (<15%) | Coronal third (15–33%) | Extends to mid-third of root & beyond | Extends to mid-third of root & beyond |
| Tooth loss (due to perio) | None | None | ≤4 teeth | ≥5 teeth |
| Complexity | Max PD ≤4mm, mostly horizontal | Max PD ≤5mm, mostly horizontal | PD ≥6mm, vertical bone loss ≥3mm, furcation Class II/III, moderate ridge defects | + masticatory dysfunction, secondary occlusal trauma (mobility ≥degree 2), severe ridge defects, bite collapse, <20 teeth (10 opposing pairs) |

— https://www.perio.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/Staging-and-Grading-Periodontitis.pdf , https://www.waterpik.com/pro/resources/pdf/AAP-classification-system-guide.pdf

**Extent descriptor** (applied to every stage): **Localized** (<30% of teeth involved), **Generalized** (≥30%), or **Molar/incisor pattern**. — same source.

**Grading** — start by assuming **Grade B**, then seek evidence to shift to A or C:

| Criterion | Grade A (slow) | Grade B (moderate) | Grade C (rapid) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Direct evidence (RBL/CAL over 5 yrs) | No loss over 5 yrs | <2 mm over 5 yrs | ≥2 mm over 5 yrs |
| Indirect: % bone loss ÷ age | <0.25 | 0.25–1.0 | >1.0 |
| Smoking (modifier) | Non-smoker | <10 cig/day | ≥10 cig/day |
| Diabetes (modifier) | Normoglycemic / no diabetes | HbA1c <7.0% | HbA1c ≥7.0% |

— https://www.perio.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/Staging-and-Grading-Periodontitis.pdf , https://www.waterpik.com/pro/resources/pdf/AAP-classification-system-guide.pdf

**Case-definition gate for periodontitis:** interdental CAL at ≥2 non-adjacent teeth, OR buccal/lingual CAL ≥3mm with pocketing >3mm at ≥2 teeth — excluding CAL from non-periodontal causes (recession trauma, caries, 3rd-molar extraction, root fracture, endo-perio lesion). — https://cdha.org/Portals/CDHA/PDF/CE%20Exams/The-ABCs-and-I-II-IIs-of-Periodontitis-Staging-and-Grading.pdf

### How leading dental/perio PMS handle perio charting UX

- **Open Dental** — captures probing depths (entered in triples e.g. `323,323`), gingival margin (signed for recession/coronal), MGJ, furcation (0–3), mobility, and plaque/calculus/bleeding/suppuration as colored dots above each probing number. **CAL is auto-calculated** (PD + GM) and shown read-only. Depths over a customizable threshold render in **red text**, and the app auto-counts red (out-of-threshold) teeth. Supports **voice charting** and configurable **auto-advance** sequencing (maxillary-first / facial-first). Displays **up to 6 most-recent exams simultaneously** (current in dark text, prior exams grayed) for longitudinal comparison. — https://www.opendental.com/manual/perio.html , https://www.opendental.com/site/0_periochart.html
- **Dentrix Voice Perio** — hands-free voice "computer assistant" that enters exam data into the Perio Chart as the clinician speaks, eliminating the need for a second person to record. Accepts spoken probing depths, bleeding, suppuration, recession, mobility, and furcation; smart-script auto-places each value in the correct location. Mic state is color-coded (blue=ready, orange=pause, red=recording). — https://blog.dentrix.com/blog/2021/07/13/voice-perio-basics/ , https://hsps.pro/Dentrix/QuickStart/Perio/dtx_00_QS_Perio_main.html
- **Dentrix exam comparison** — `Options > Exam Comparison` compares **up to four perio exams at once**, selectable per measurement (probing depths, attachment level, etc.) in a side-by-side view to show patient progress/regression; exams can be exported to PDF. — https://blog.dentrixenterprise.com/comparing-perio-exam-information/ , https://learn.dentrixascend.com/courses/clinical-essentials-for-teams/lessons/perio-charting/topic/entering-a-perio-exam/
- **Curve Dental** — simplifies entry/evaluation of pocket depth, gingival margin, bleeding, calculus, suppuration, furcation, and mobility. — https://www.curvedental.com/perio-charting

### Perio chart comparison over time

Longitudinal comparison is a first-class feature in best-in-class systems: Open Dental renders the **6 most-recent exams in one grid** (current dark, prior grayed) and Dentrix offers an explicit **Exam Comparison** mode for up to 4 exams selectable per metric — both to surface trend/progress and to drive the patient conversation. This validates dentalemon's snapshot/carousel concept: the clinical norm is multi-exam visual comparison with threshold (red-line) emphasis on out-of-range sites. — https://www.opendental.com/manual/perio.html , https://blog.dentrixenterprise.com/comparing-perio-exam-information/

## Completeness gaps to check in our product

Checklist of perio data points + journeys a best-in-class perio chart captures. Audit dentalemon's perio module against each:

**Per-tooth / per-site data capture**
- [ ] **6-point probing depths** per tooth (MB, B, DB, ML, L, DL) — full mouth, not 1 or 4 points
- [ ] **Bleeding on Probing (BOP)** recorded **per site** (not per tooth), including at shallow ≤3mm sites
- [ ] **Suppuration** marker per site
- [ ] **Plaque** marker per site + whole-mouth plaque-index %
- [ ] **Calculus** marker per site
- [ ] **Gingival margin / recession** as a **signed** value relative to CEJ (negative = recession, positive = coronal)
- [ ] **Clinical Attachment Level (CAL)** — **auto-calculated** from PD + GM per site, shown read-only; correct formula for at-CEJ / recession / coronal-to-CEJ cases
- [ ] **Mobility** per tooth using **Miller N/1/2/3** scale (with >1mm = Grade 2, depressible = Grade 3)
- [ ] **Furcation** per furcation site using **Glickman I–IV** (and/or **Hamp 1–3**); only on multi-rooted teeth
- [ ] **MGJ position / keratinized-attached gingiva width**

**Diagnosis / classification**
- [ ] **% BOP** auto-computed and bucketed (health <10%, localized 10–30%, generalized >30%)
- [ ] **2017 AAP/EFP Stage (I–IV)** computed/assisted from CAL (worst site), RBL, tooth loss, and complexity factors (PD, vertical bone loss, furcation II/III, mobility ≥2, bite collapse, <20 teeth)
- [ ] **2017 AAP/EFP Grade (A/B/C)** from progression (RBL/CAL over 5 yr), % bone loss ÷ age, plus **smoking** and **diabetes/HbA1c** modifiers
- [ ] **Extent descriptor** (localized <30% / generalized ≥30% / molar-incisor pattern)
- [ ] Captures grading inputs (smoking status, HbA1c) — likely sourced from medical history, not the chart itself

**Visualization & UX**
- [ ] **Color-coded depth visualization** with a configurable **red-line threshold** (out-of-range sites highlighted; count of affected teeth)
- [ ] **Comparison to prior chart(s)** — multi-exam side-by-side (target ≥4–6 exams), trend/progress indicators; maps directly to our snapshot/carousel concept
- [ ] **Voice-driven / hands-free entry** with auto-advance sequencing (solo clinician workflow without a recorder)
- [ ] **Auto-advance / keyboard entry** sequence (e.g. enter triples, maxillary-first or facial-first ordering)
- [ ] **Export / share** a perio exam as PDF for referral/patient

**Likely gaps to scrutinize first (highest clinical-credibility value):**
1. CAL **auto-calc** correctness across all three GM/CEJ cases (most-cited differentiator).
2. **2017 AAP staging/grading** support — modern PMS increasingly compute/assist this; absence is a credibility gap for a perio-serious product.
3. **6-point** completeness + **per-site BOP/suppuration** (not per-tooth aggregation).
4. **Multi-exam comparison** depth/threshold visualization (anchors the snapshot/carousel value prop).
5. **Voice / hands-free** entry — table-stakes for the solo-hygienist journey in Dentrix/Open Dental.
