# Visit Workspace + Dental Charting — Industry Research

Benchmark of leading dental PMS products (Dentrix, Open Dental, Curve Dental, CareStack, tab32, Eaglesoft, Denticon) and clinical charting norms, to evaluate our visit workspace + odontogram ("carousel") module.

## Industry-standard benchmark

### Per-visit clinical workspace structure
- The dominant pattern is a **Chart module** that combines a graphical tooth chart, a procedure/progress-notes list, a treatment-entry panel, and clinical notes on one screen — the clinician charts findings and treatment from the same view. In Open Dental the Chart module contains the Graphical Tooth Chart, an Enter Treatment tab, Progress Notes, and a Show tab for filtering (https://www.opendental.com/manual/chart.html, https://opendental.com/manual/entertreatment.html).
- **Treatment planning is a distinct-but-linked surface**: planned procedures are charted in the same odontogram but managed/prioritized in a separate Treatment Plan module (Open Dental: https://www.opendental.com/manual/treatmentplan.html; Dentrix Treatment Planner organizes "treatment cases, case status and severity, treatment visits": https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3MYyHnW_oD8). Table-stakes: a procedure charted as planned can be sequenced into visits and converted to completed.
- **Clinical notes attached to procedures**, not just the patient — notes can be general or tied to a specific procedure (Dentrix: https://hsps.pro/Dentrix/Help/mergedProjects/Chart/Patient_Chart_overview.htm).
- **Voice/AI charting is the current differentiator frontier**: Dentrix markets "clinical notes that write themselves" via voice (https://www.dentrix.com/dental-solutions/charting-imaging-and-clinical-ai/streamline-clinical-charting/); Bola AI + Curve Dental ship "Voice Perio" hands-free perio charting to Curve's user base (https://bola.ai/news/bola-ai-and-curve-dental-partner-to-integrate-voice-charting/); tab32 differentiates on voice charting + voice perio that "Curve Dental does not offer" (https://tab32.com/tab32-compared-to-curve-dental/).
- **Cloud, all-in-one** (scheduling + charting + imaging + billing in one workspace) is now table-stakes among newer entrants — CareStack and tab32 both position on it (https://tab32.com/, https://carestack.com/dental-software/compare).

### Tooth notation standards
- Three live systems: **Universal Numbering System** (permanent 1–32, primary A–T) is the **US standard** ("commonly used only in the United States… the American system"); **FDI / ISO 3950** two-digit (quadrant digit 1–4 permanent, 5–8 primary; second digit = position) is "used worldwide" and is the international standard; **Palmer** (1–8 with quadrant brackets, primary A–E) is largely superseded but "overwhelmingly continues to be the preferred method… in the United Kingdom" and in orthodontics (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dental_notation).
- A fourth, **Haderup**, is used primarily in Denmark (https://www.facebook.com/HowardFarran/posts/...; corroborated in Open Dental which lists Universal/FDI/Haderup/Palmer as selectable).
- **UX norm: notation is a configurable display preference, not hard-coded.** Open Dental supports all four and lets the chart render in the practice's chosen system (Universal, FDI, Haderup, Palmer) (https://www.opendental.com/manual/graphicaltoothchart.html). Best-in-class products let international deployments switch system without changing the underlying tooth identity.

### Charting conventions (surfaces, status, dentition)
- **Five surfaces** with standard letters: **M**esial, **D**istal, **O**cclusal (or **I**ncisal on anteriors), **B**uccal (or **La**bial), **L**ingual (or **P**alatal); multi-surface restorations are recorded as combinations (MO, DO, MOD, MI) (https://dentalreviewed.com/blog/step-by-step-dental-charting-for-professionals). A procedure's "paint type and treatment area" determine which surfaces/graphic render on the tooth (https://www.opendental.com/manual/graphicaltoothchart.html).
- **Status layers are first-class.** Open Dental's chart filters by five procedure statuses: **TP** (treatment planned), **C** (completed), **EO/EC** (existing — other/current provider), **R** (referred out), and **Cn** (condition); any combination can be toggled to control what's visible (https://opendental.blog/feature-highlight-chart-module-show-tab/). Dentrix similarly distinguishes charting **existing** treatment from planned/completed (https://learn.dentrixascend.com/courses/clinical-essentials-for-teams/lessons/charting/topic/charting-existing-treatment/).
- **Existing-vs-planned-vs-completed must be visually distinct.** Conventional color coding: **blue = existing/completed**, **red = treatment-needed/planned**; conditions use symbols (X = missing/extracted, circle = extraction needed, line through root = RCT done) (https://dentalreviewed.com/blog/step-by-step-dental-charting-for-professionals). Open Dental colors are driven by procedure status (treatment-planned, complete, existing-other-provider, existing-current-provider, referred, condition) (https://www.opendental.com/manual/graphicaltoothchart.html).
- **Mixed dentition is required, not optional.** Products must show primary + permanent teeth simultaneously and switch a tooth between dentitions: Eaglesoft "Mixed Dentition" displays permanent and primary on one screen (https://pattersonsupport.custhelp.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/1668/...); Carestream/SoftDent lets you chart permanent teeth on a juvenile and primary teeth on an adult (https://help.carestreamdental.com/.../Charting_Mixed_Dentition.htm); Dentrix Ascend supports selecting teeth and changing them to permanent dentition (https://blog.dentrixascend.com/2020/10/28/changing-dentition-in-the-tooth-chart/). Pediatric guidance explicitly calls for "quick switching between views" of primary/mixed/permanent (https://www.oryxdental.com/choose-pediatric-dental-charting-tools/).

### Chart history / longitudinal comparison UX (core to our carousel)
- The established pattern is a **date slider under the tooth chart**: "Drag the slider bar under the tooth chart" to show historical changes / replay how the dentition evolved over time (https://www.opendental.com/manual/chart.html, https://www.opendental.com/manual/graphicaltoothchart.html). Planned procedures render as of their TP date; other statuses as of their entry date — i.e., the chart is a **time-indexed projection** of procedure events, which validates a snapshot/timeline model (https://www.opendental.com/manual/graphicaltoothchart.html).
- Beyond the date-slider, longitudinal comparison is most mature in **imaging** (before/after image comparison) and in **perio** (comparing pocket-depth exams across dates); a dedicated odontogram before/after carousel is **not** a standard, ubiquitous feature — it is closer to a differentiator than table-stakes.

### Clinical/charting accuracy expectations and pitfalls
- Charting is a **legal/ethical record obligation**; accuracy and completeness are expected (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3970394/).
- Documented inaccuracy is high on paper: a UK audit of 1,128 records found inaccuracy ranging 16%–83% across practices (https://www.dental-update.co.uk/content/general-dental-practice/inaccurate-dental-charting-in-an-audit-of-1128-general-dental-practice-records); a separate study found ~44% of UK charts contained inaccuracies — wrong-tooth restorations, miscounts, omissions (https://dentalreviewed.com/blog/step-by-step-dental-charting-for-professionals).
- **Common error classes** the UI must defend against: left/right confusion, charting on an adjacent tooth, delayed entry (memory lapse), inconsistent symbols across staff, and wrong-tooth/wrong-surface entry (https://dentalreviewed.com/blog/step-by-step-dental-charting-for-professionals; veterinary parallel: https://learnvetdentistry.com/the-importance-of-accurate-dental-charting-in-veterinary-medicine/). Electronic charting is cited as the mitigation, but only if it constrains/validates input (https://resources.carestack.com/article/everything-to-know-about-dental-charting-in-2023).
- **Audit trail + signing + provider attribution** are expected for a defensible record: Open Dental logs creation, sign, reopen, signature-invalidation and edits per user, gated by permissions (https://www.opendental.com/manual/audittrail.html, https://opendental.com/manual/permissions.html).

## Completeness gaps to check in our product

A best-in-class visit workspace + odontogram should cover all of the below. Verify our coverage (relevant code: `apps/dentalemon/src/features/workspace/components/dental-chart.tsx`, `five-surface-selector.tsx`, `timeline-carousel.tsx`, `dental/universal-tooth.tsx`, `dental/universal-tooth-fdi.tsx`, `hooks/use-initialize-dentition.ts`, `hooks/use-save-tooth-flow.ts`).

- [ ] **Notation switching** — Universal (US) AND FDI/ISO 3950 (international) selectable as a display preference, with tooth identity stable underneath; consider Palmer for UK/ortho. (We have universal-tooth + universal-tooth-fdi — confirm a user-facing switch + locale default, not dev-only.)
- [ ] **Five-surface charting** — M/D/O(I)/B(La)/L(P) selectable, with multi-surface combinations (MO, MOD); surface set adapts for anterior (incisal/labial) vs posterior teeth.
- [ ] **Status layers** — existing (own vs other provider), planned/treatment-planned, completed, referred-out, and condition — each visually distinct and independently filterable (toggle which layers show on the chart).
- [ ] **Existing-vs-planned-vs-completed lifecycle** — a planned procedure can be sequenced into a visit and transitioned to completed; status transitions are enforced/auditable (note our known treatment state machine: diagnosed→planned→performed).
- [ ] **Conditions/findings vocabulary** — missing/extracted, RCT, crown, caries, watch, etc., with standard symbols/colors (blue existing/done, red planned/needed).
- [ ] **Mixed dentition** — simultaneous primary + permanent display, primary teeth on adults and permanent on juveniles, and per-tooth switching of dentition; pediatric "quick view switch" (primary / mixed / permanent).
- [ ] **Chart history / time travel** — a date slider or equivalent to replay the chart as of any date; chart state is a time-indexed projection of dated procedure events (our carousel/snapshot model — confirm it spans full chart, not just one tooth).
- [ ] **Longitudinal comparison** — per-tooth and whole-chart before/after comparison across snapshots; align with imaging before/after and perio exam-over-time patterns.
- [ ] **Undo / correction safety** — easy reversal of a mis-charted tooth/surface BEFORE finalize, plus correction-with-audit AFTER finalize (guards against wrong-tooth/adjacent-tooth errors).
- [ ] **Wrong-entry defenses** — left/right orientation cues, confirm-on-destructive-action (extraction/missing), and constraints that prevent invalid surface/tooth combinations.
- [ ] **Multi-provider attribution** — every charted entry records the performing/charting provider; "existing — other provider" vs "existing — current provider" distinction.
- [ ] **Audit trail + signing** — immutable log of chart edits (who/when/what), note/chart signing, and signature-invalidation on edit; permission-gated.
- [ ] **Clinical notes tied to procedures** — notes attachable to a specific charted procedure, not only the patient; templated/auto notes per procedure.
- [ ] **Treatment plan sequencing** — planned procedures grouped into cases/visits with prioritization and case status/severity.
- [ ] **Voice/AI charting (differentiator)** — hands-free charting / auto-generated notes; competitive frontier (Dentrix, Curve+Bola, tab32). Optional but rising to expected.
- [ ] **Perio charting parity** — pocket depths, bleeding, recession, mobility with exam-over-time comparison (separate from odontogram but expected in the same workspace).
